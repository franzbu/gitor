February

* February 2: "To engage in actions without desire for their fruit is true tyaga (renunciation). God is the Divine Renunciant... Anyone aspiring to Self-realization... must act and live for the Lord without being emotionally involved." — Paramahansa Yogananda, God Talks With Arjuna: The Bhagavad Gita
* February 3: "The saints stress nonattachment so that one strong point of material attachment may not prevent our attaining the entire kingdom of God. Renunciation does not mean giving up everything; it means giving up small pleasures for eternal bliss." — Paramahansa Yogananda, How You Can Talk With God
* February 4: "Renunciation is not an end, it is the means to an end. The real renunciant is he who lives for God first, regardless of his outer mode of existence." — Paramahansa Yogananda, Man's Eternal Quest

* February 7: "Renunciation is the wise path trod by the devotee who willingly gives up the lesser for the greater... No one should fear the rigors of self-denial; the spiritual blessings that follow are great and incomparable." — Paramahansa Yogananda, God Talks With Arjuna: The Bhagavad Gita
* February 8: "If I ever felt a little human wish for something I could not have, this question was immediately presented to my soul, 'What did you come [to the Guru's ashram] for?' The answer was always, 'For God alone.'" — Sri Gyanamata, God Alone: The Life and Letters of a Saint
* February 9: "Renunciation is not negative but positive. It isn't the giving up of anything except misery... Rather it is a divine investment, by which our few cents of self-discipline will yield a million spiritual dollars." — Paramahansa Yogananda, Sayings of Paramahansa Yogananda
* February 10: "The Lord wants us to escape this delusive world... You must seek His love, for it encompasses eternal freedom, endless joy, and immortality." — Paramahansa Yogananda, The Divine Romance

May
   * May 1: "The only way to attain salvation is to have complete loyalty to God. This dream of life will be taken away from you one day; the only thing that is real is the love of God. Nothing else; all are false dreams. Get away from them. Every minute I see how necessary that is. He has tied me to the SRF work, and so I tell Him: 'I shall work for You alone.' Then I feel within His supreme joy." — Paramahansa Yogananda, The Divine Romance
 
   * May 2: "Make the effort to please God first. It is impossible to please all. I try never to displease anybody. I do my best and that is all I can do. My first aim is to please God. I use my hands to pray in adoration before Him, my feet to seek Him everywhere, my mind to think of Him as always present. Every throne of thought must be occupied by God—God as peace, God as love, God as kindness, God as understanding, God as compassion, God as wisdom. This is the only thing that I have come to tell you. Naught else." — Paramahansa Yogananda, Man's Eternal Quest
 
   * May 3: "In the spiritually receptive, loyalty to the guru rises even when the disciple’s heart is bathed in the aura of the guru’s unconditional love. The soul knows that it has found at last a true friend, counselor, and guide. The disciple strives therefore to reciprocate the guru’s unconditional love, especially when tested, even as the faith and loyalty of Jesus’ disciples were often tried with nonunderstanding. Many were with Jesus at the feasts and sermons, but how few at the cross!" — Paramahansa Yogananda, The Second Coming of Christ
 
   * May 4: "I take a sacred vow: Never shall the sun gaze of my love sink below the horizon of my thought of Thee. Never will I lower the vision of my lifted eyes to place it on aught but Thee. Never will I do anything that reminds me not of Thee." — Paramahansa Yogananda, Whispers from Eternity
 
   * May 5: "All that I have to give to you is the spirit of Master and of God... I shall never be more than a little one because it will always be Master, Paramahansaji, who is my life and my blessing to you all." — Rajarsi Janakananda, Rajarsi Janakananda: A Great Western Yogi
 
   * May 6: "True devotees may be called fanatical in their devotion to Him. The only right kind of fanaticism is loyalty to God—night and day, night and day, thinking of Him. I shall try to feel that love today for everyone—friend and foe alike." — Paramahansa Yogananda, Journey to Self-realization
                     * December 26: "After the outer celebrations are over, let not your spiritual intensity wane. The spirit of Christmas—the spirit of selfless love—should be maintained throughout the year. Every day is a day for the birth of Christ within you." — Paramahansa Yogananda, Self-Realization Magazine
                     * December 27: "As the year draws to a close, look back not with regret for your mistakes, but with gratitude for the lessons learned. Each experience, whether it seemed happy or sad, was a step toward your ultimate goal of God-realization." — Paramahansa Yogananda, SRF Lessons
                     * December 28: "Do not let the closing of the year be a time of dissipation. Use these final days to deepen your meditation and to center your mind once more upon the Infinite. The quiet before the new year is a precious time for communion." — Paramahansa Yogananda, Man's Eternal Quest
                     * December 29: "Take a final inventory of your spiritual life. Have you grown closer to God this year? Have you mastered any of your bad habits? If not, do not be discouraged; resolve that the coming year will be a year of dynamic spiritual progress." — Paramahansa Yogananda, The Law of Success
                     * December 30: "When you enter the New Year, resolve that you will not be the same person you were before. You will be a new being, reborn in the wisdom and love of God, more capable of serving Him and all His children." — Paramahansa Yogananda, Self-Realization Magazine
                     * December 31: "At the end of this year, I lay all my failures and successes at the feet of the Divine. I ask only that in the coming year, I may be a clearer channel for His love, His wisdom, and His peace. I am ready to begin anew." — Paramahansa Yogananda, Whispers from Eternity
